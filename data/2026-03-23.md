### 04:51 @unknown

Basic RAG searches once and answers. Agentic RAG thinks, searches multiple times, verifies, and then answers. An AI agent sits on top of RAG and makes the decisions.  In basic RAG, we give a query, it retrieves relevant documents from a knowledge base, and the LLM generates an answer. One search, one answer. But sometimes one search is not enough.  In Agentic RAG, the agent decides what to search, when to search, and from where to search. It can break a complex question into sub-questions, search different sources for each, evaluate if the retrieved information is sufficient, and search again with a refined query if needed.  This is how Agentic RAG comes into the picture when basic RAG is not enough for complex queries.
