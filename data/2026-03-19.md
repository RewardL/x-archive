Here's something nobody in the AI video space is talking about.  The entire AI industry is extraordinarily compute-constrained right now. (That's not news).  What IS interesting is the economics of where that compute goes.  Serving LLM text models is VASTLY more profitable than serving video generation models. It's not even close.  So what do the major labs do? They pour compute infrastructure and R&D into the cash cow. Text-based models. Because that's where the margins are and that market has a looooong way to run.  This has a massive downstream effect on the video application layer.  Video inference is going to improve much more slowly than people expect. The models are still pretty crappy, let's be honest. (Especially the 10s clip gen limits). And the labs have VERY little economic incentive to change that right now.  Here's where it gets interesting for builders.  As token-based LLM use cases get more saturated, and more importantly as compute infrastructure actually scales up (which takes YEARS, not months), the model companies will slowly redirect resources toward video serving and video R&D. They have to. It's the next revenue frontier.  But that rotation is going to be gradual. Not a switch flip.  Which means the video application layer has a window. A real one.  If you're building in AI video right now, the slow pace of model improvement is not the enemy. It's the moat. You get to build distribution, lock in workflows, and own the customer relationship while the underlying tech is still catching up.  By the time video inference is actually great, the distribution game is already over.
